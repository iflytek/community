## Purpose and Summary

This policy outlines which incident response team has jurisdiction to resolve reported Code of Conduct (CoC) violations:

* The Code of Conduct Committee
* Project-level Code of Conduct responders

In general:

* Project-level CoC responders resolve incidents that occur entirely within the project, with some exceptions.
* The CoC Committee resolves incidents that are project-agnostic, impact multiple projects, or that occur in projects that do not have their own designated CoC responders, with some exceptions.

## Jurisdiction of Project CoC Responders

Projects that have their own designated Code of Conduct (CoC) responders have jurisdiction over alleged violations that occur entirely within the project, except when transfer of an incident is required below.

### Transfer by Project CoC Responders

Project-level Code of Conduct responders shall promptly transfer a reported incident to the CoC Committee if any of the following are true:

* The incident is project-agnostic or impacts multiple projects.
* A majority of project-level Code of Conduct responders have hard conflicts of interest (as described in the [Conflict of Interest Policy](coc-incident-resolution-procedures.md#conflicts-of-interest)).
* Laws or regulations may have been violated.
* The incident or its resolution is likely to expose the community or project leaders to legal risk or liability.

### Appeal of Project Decisions

An involved party (a reporter, a person who was targeted or harmed by the incident, or the accused person) can appeal a decision of project-level CoC responders to the CoC Committee. The CoC Committee may reverse or overturn a Project-level decision regarding a CoC incident only in the following circumstances:

* The Project-level CoC responders lacked jurisdiction and were required to transfer the incident to the CoC Committee per "Transfer by Project CoC Responders" above;
* Individuals who have hard conflicts of interest (see the [Conflict of Interest Policy](coc-incident-resolution-procedures.md#conflicts-of-interest)) decided the outcome of the incident;
* The Project-level responders did not conduct a fair evaluation of the incident (e.g., they failed to review relevant evidence that was available to them);
* The Project-level responders violated applicable policies or procedures when responding to or resolving the incident;
* The outcome is grossly disproportionate to the alleged misconduct or grossly inadequate to protect the community from further harm.

## Jurisdiction of the CoC Committee

The CoC Committee has jurisdiction over alleged violations of the [Community Code of Conduct](https://github.com/iflytek/community/blob/main/code-of-conduct.md) that meet any of the following criteria:

* The incident occurred in a project that does not have its own project-level Code of Conduct enforcement team.
* The incident is project-agnostic or impacts multiple projects.
* The incident was transferred to the CoC Committee by a project-level responder.

If an incident is reported to the CoC Committee that falls under the jurisdiction of project-level responders, the committee will transfer resolution of the incident to the project-level responders.

## Central Recordkeeping and Notification

To ensure that there is a central repository of data regarding Code of Conduct incidents across all projects that can be checked to determine whether an accused person is a repeat offender, Project-level incident responders shall promptly notify the CoC Committee of any incident that is reported to them even if it is not transferred. The Project-level incident responders shall provide a brief summary of the nature of the alleged violation and the name of the accused person (but do not have to provide a copy of the full incident report or the identities of the reporters, witnesses, or persons allegedly harmed or targeted), and the CoC Committee shall in response notify the project-level Code of Conduct responders if the accused individual has a record of prior violations. All information in the central repository shall remain confidential under the Confidentiality Policy.
