This document outlines the governance role hierarchy and the responsibilities associated with each role in the Iflytek Opensource Community. 

## Community Members

Community members are anyone who interacts with the project in any way. This includes:

* Asking or answering questions on mailing lists, Slack, or forums
* Filing or commenting on issues
* Reviewing or commenting on pull requests
* Attending community meetings

## Contributors

Contributors are anyone who contributes code, documentation, or other artifacts to the project. This includes:

* Submitting pull requests
* Reviewing code
* Writing documentation
* Participating in community discussions

We encourage all contributors to become members. We aim to grow an active, healthy community of contributors, reviewers, and code owners.

## Organization Members

Organization members are contributors who have been granted write access to the community GitHub organization. Requirements:

* Enabled two-factor authentication on their GitHub account
* Have made at least **10 contributions** (issues, pull requests, comments, etc.) to the community
* Have been actively contributing for at least **1 month**
* Have an active [CLA](CLA.md) on file

To become an organization member, submit a membership request by [filing an issue](https://github.com/iflytek/community/issues/new).

Organization members are expected to:

* Follow the [Code of Conduct](code-of-conduct.md)
* Follow the project's [governance](governance.md)
* Actively contribute to the community

## Reviewers

Reviewers are organization members who have demonstrated good judgment in code review. Requirements:

* Be an organization member
* Be actively contributing to the subproject
* Have completed at least **20 substantial reviews** (not authored by self) of PRs in the subproject

Reviewers are expected to:

* Provide thorough and thoughtful code reviews
* Help maintain code quality
* Mentor new contributors

## Approvers

Approvers are reviewers who have demonstrated deep knowledge of the subproject and the ability to approve changes. Requirements:

* Be a reviewer for the subproject
* Have reviewed and commented on at least **30 PRs** (not authored by self)
* Have authored or reviewed and approved at least **10 PRs**

Approvers are expected to:

* Approve or reject pull requests
* Provide technical leadership
* Ensure code quality and consistency
* Mentor reviewers and contributors

## Subproject Owners

Subproject owners are approvers who have overall responsibility for a subproject. They are the technical leaders of the subproject. Requirements:

* Be an approver for the subproject
* Have demonstrated deep knowledge of the subproject
* Have contributed to the subproject's direction and roadmap

Subproject owners are expected to:

* Define the subproject's technical vision
* Make final decisions on technical matters within the subproject
* Approve major changes to the subproject
* Mentor approvers and reviewers

## Membership Changes

Membership levels are granted based on demonstrated contributions and merit. If you believe you meet the requirements for a higher level of membership, please reach out to the relevant SIG leads or file an issue.

If a member becomes inactive for an extended period, their membership level may be reduced. Reactivation is possible upon resuming contributions.
